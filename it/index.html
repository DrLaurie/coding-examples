What is this all about, anyway?

Back in 1983, I was learning BASIC programming on my grandfather's Commodore VIC-20, and I was excited to find a book called Volcanic Games for Your VIC-20 by Hal Renko and Sam Edwards in our public library. One of the programs in the book was called I.T.: The Intraterrestrial, and it was a fantasy text adventure game in a style similar to The Hobbit. Of course, being a nine-year-old with a short attention span, I never finished typing out the program....

Thirty-six years later, I discovered an online archive of old VIC-20 programs which included I.T.: The Intraterrestrial. I was determined to finally play the game and see how it ends, but no longer having access to my trusty VIC-20, I had to convert everything from a procedural programming language (BASIC) to an object oriented programming language (JavaScript). This was made even more challenging by the variable names in the original program; BASIC had a two-character limit for variable names, so I found myself guessing at what the original authors had intended for some of the variables. (What is TH? Or HL?)

After spending most of the Easter holiday weekend coding (and forcing my gamer husband to be a reluctant tester), I am proud to say that I finally have a working beta version. There may still be a few glitches, but at least I can finally say that I have succeeded at defusing the Nullity Bomb and saving the world, 36 years later.

— Dr. Laurie Alfaro, April 21, 2019 (close)
